# ABS Challenge System (2026) — ops notes for MLB-BOIZ

Primary sources:

- [MLB: ABS Challenge System starting in 2026](https://www.mlb.com/news/abs-challenge-system-mlb-2026)
- [Baseball Savant — ABS dashboard](https://baseballsavant.mlb.com/abs)
- [Savant — ABS challenge leaderboard](https://baseballsavant.mlb.com/leaderboard/abs-challenges)

## What shipped in MLB 2026

- **Not** full robot umpires on every pitch. **Human** home-plate umpire calls balls/strikes live.
- **Challenge system**: only **batter, pitcher, or catcher** may challenge; **tap cap/helmet** immediately after the pitch.
- **Two challenges per team** to start; wrong twice and challenges are gone; **successful challenge retains** the challenge.
- **Every MLB park** except special events without infrastructure (e.g. Mexico City Series, Field of Dreams, Little League Classic). **Postseason: yes.**

## Strike zone definition (ABS review)

From MLB’s article:

- **2D zone** (not the rulebook 3D volume). A 3D prototype was rejected because edge-nicking breaking balls produced “wrong” strikes.
- Ball is judged where it crosses the **middle of the plate** (depth described as **8.5 inches from front and back to center** — i.e. **mid-plane**, not the broadcast front plane).
- Top/bottom of zone keyed to **measured player height** (no cleats): top **53.5%**, bottom **27%**; width **17 inches**.

MLB’s testing summary: ABS zone vs typical human zone — humans historically a bit **more pitcher-friendly up** and **tighter down** than the ABS rectangle (see MLB article for the cited height percentages).

## Live stats (Savant dashboard)

The Savant ABS page tracks challenge attempts, overturn rates, and splits (batters vs fielders). Use it for **process** metrics (who challenges well) — not the same as league K% causal attribution (small sample + behavior change).

## Framing

Full ABS (auto call every pitch) would kill framing. The **challenge hybrid** preserves framing except on challenged pitches — catcher value shifts toward **challenge judgment** as well as receiving.

## Betting / modeling implications

- **Catcher framing runs saved** is a weaker standalone thesis under challenge ABS; consider **challenge value** and **borderline pitch profiles** instead.
- Any “geometry edge” thesis must use **mid-plane** ABS definition, not “front of plate only” (MLB explicitly moved away from front-plane for slow curves clipping the lip then dying).
